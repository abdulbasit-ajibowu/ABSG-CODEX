Core Mandate & Mission

The Ministry is the government body responsible for planning, implementing, and monitoring public health policies in Abia State. Its key functions include raising health awareness, ensuring healthcare access, and safeguarding the quality of services across both urban and rural settings.

Vision & Mission

  • Vision: To lead in realizing "good health for all" Abia citizens.
  • Mission: To align, develop, and implement health policies and programs that benefit Abians — leveraging partnerships, technology, strategy, and networks to deliver impactful interventions and strengthen the health system.

Project Ekwueme (May 2025):

This flagship health intervention aims to strengthen primary healthcare across the state via a Build–Equip–Employ (BEE) strategy. It targets the renovation and equipping of 200 PHCs (ensuring one per ward), staffing them, and upgrading three general hospitals as referral centers. It also rebrands the Nursing School into a College to boost training capacity.

Health Insurance for Civil Servants (April–August 2025)

A formal scheme launched to reduce out-of-pocket costs for civil servants. The government pays half the contribution for the first three months. The program includes coverage for dependants and promises upgrades to key medical facilities like ABSUTH and Amachara General Hospital.

Cholera Response (June 2024)

The Ministry declared Abia cholera-free after investigating 109 suspected cases across six LGAs and finding zero confirmed infections.
